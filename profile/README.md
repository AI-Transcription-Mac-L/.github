## 📖 Overview

<a href="#aITranscription">AI Transcription</a> brings OpenAI's Whisper speech recognition model to Mac as a native application — converting audio and video files to text with state-of-the-art accuracy, multiple language support, and complete local processing that keeps audio content private without cloud upload.

The <a href="#aITranscription">Whisper AI accuracy</a> is the foundation of AI Transcription's value — OpenAI's Whisper model represents a significant advance in speech recognition accuracy compared to earlier generation transcription tools, handling diverse accents, technical vocabulary, background noise, and varied audio quality with substantially better results than previous open-source and commercial transcription solutions. The model processes audio through deep learning trained on a large multilingual dataset, producing transcripts that require notably less manual correction than earlier transcription tools on typical audio quality. The <a href="#aITranscription">local processing architecture</a> runs the Whisper model entirely on the Mac without sending audio to any cloud service — all speech-to-text conversion happens locally using Mac CPU and GPU resources. This privacy-preserving design means confidential interviews, private meetings, sensitive business discussions, and any other audio content can be transcribed without the privacy exposure of cloud-based transcription services that upload audio to remote servers.

The <a href="#aITranscription">100-plus language support</a> transcribes audio in a wide range of languages automatically — Whisper detects the spoken language automatically or the user specifies it explicitly, producing transcripts in the language of the audio with the same model architecture across all supported languages. The <a href="#aITranscription">timestamped transcript output</a> produces text with timing information indicating when in the audio each segment was spoken — enabling precise navigation between transcript text and the corresponding audio position, essential for journalist workflows, legal transcription review, and content editing that requires verifying specific sections.</p>

<a href="#aITranscription">SRT subtitle export</a> formats transcribed text as subtitle files compatible with video players, video editing software, and publishing platforms — converting a transcription session directly to subtitles for video content without a separate subtitle creation step. <a href="#aITranscription">Speaker identification labeling</a> attempts to distinguish between different speakers in multi-speaker audio — labeling transcript segments by speaker for interviews, meetings, and conversations with multiple participants.

<a href="#aITranscription">Microphone live transcription</a> converts speech to text in real time from the Mac microphone — transcribing meetings, interviews, lectures, and any live audio as it occurs rather than requiring post-processing of a recording. <a href="#aITranscription">Transcript editing interface</a> provides text editing capabilities within the application — correcting recognition errors, adjusting speaker labels, and editing the transcript before export without requiring export to a separate text editor for corrections.</p>
